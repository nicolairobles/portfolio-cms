In addition to providing the core measurement for television, metered household panels act as truth sets, or calibration sources, for big data sets that often do not have any demographic information associated with them—we’ll talk about this point a bit more in the “Census-based measurement” section later on. Panelists are extensively trained to understand how to use the Nielsen meters and are thoroughly interviewed to gather demographic information on the household.

Nielsen uses a variety of panels and methods to measure TV viewing. The heart of this measurement is the use of real consumers who provide a representative view of media consumption habits today.
