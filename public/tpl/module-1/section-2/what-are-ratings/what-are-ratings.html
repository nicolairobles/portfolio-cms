What are Ratings?

So once we’re able to measure who is consuming content and ads across screens, the next question is, “What do buyers and sellers use to see how well they did?” Let’s take a look at what types of metrics media buyers and sellers use to sell and evaluate their media.

Advertisers use a ton of metrics to inform their messaging strategies. Ratings provide the most well-known metric—the number of people exposed to a piece of content or ad. Remember, ratings are represented as a percentage of a specific population or audience—they’re often referred to as “the currency” of the media industry because they are used to price advertising. Once an ad campaign has run, media buyers and sellers review ratings reports to determine if their media reached the intended audience.
