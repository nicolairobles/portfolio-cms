Primary Playback Periods

Time frame is very important when we talk about ratings. Media buyers and sellers want to know when people are exposed to content and for how long. This is particularly relevant at a time when consumers can access content from a number of platforms at any time they choose. In the video and audio realm, we call this the playback period. Generally speaking, this refers to the window of time that an audience is engaged with content.

Live

You were exposed to the media when it was originally broadcast

Live + Same Day

You were exposed to the media when it was originally broadcast or at some point that same day

Live + 3 Days

You were exposed to the media when it was originally broadcast or at some point within three days of that original broadcast

Live + 7 Days

You were exposed to the media when it was originally broadcast or at some point within seven days of that original broadcast

Live + X Days

Because media is available to consumers for quite a bit longer than 7 days nowadays, buyers/sellers oftentimes look at a large timeframe to see what types of people interacted with their media throughout its lifespan
