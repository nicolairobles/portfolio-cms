Who are key players in the media industry?

Let's go over one last thing before we end this section. There are two main parties that use ratings data within the media industry: media buyers and media sellers.

Media Buyers

Media buyers are parties that buy advertising space to market products and/or services. There are two primary types of media buyers: advertisers and agencies.

  • ADVERTISER: Companies that want to market their products and services for purchase.
  • AGENCY: Companies that advise advertisers on how and where to market their products and services.
Media Sellers

Media sellers that sell advertising space within various content.

There are five key media sellers in the media industry: TV networks, digital publishers, radio broadcasters, ad networks/platforms and multichannel video programming distributors.

  • TV NETWORK: An organization that distributes programming to TV stations/cable networks and content providers.
  • DIGITAL PUBLISHER: Media owners of sites and/or apps that publish content for consumption.
  • RADIO BROADCASTER: An organization that distributes radio programming to stations and content providers.
  • AD NETWORK/PLATFORM: Aggregates online ad spots across multiple digital sites. Ad networks and platforms help publishers find the best ad spots given their advertiserâ€™s demand. They also offer advertisers online ad spots sold in bulk to reach their target audience online.
  • MULTICHANNEL VIDEO PROGRAMMING DISTRIBUTOR (MVPD): An organization that delivers video programming services, usually for a subscription fee (pay television). These providers include direct broadcast satellite (DBS) providers, cable television (CATV) systems and other wireline video providers (including Verizon FiOS, AT&T, U-verse) and competitive local exchange carriers (CLECs) using internet protocol television (IPTV).
