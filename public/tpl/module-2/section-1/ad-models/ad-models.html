Ad Models

There are two models for ads.

LINEAR ADVERTISING

A linear ad is one that is distributed to many people. Specifically, an ad (or series of ads) is/are delivered to an audience in a set order at a set time determined by the media owner. So the entire audience is exposed to the same ad or set of ads.

You’ll oftentimes see linear ads while watching your 8 PM show on TV. That shampoo commercial you just saw was also seen by your aunt watching the same show down the street.

DYNAMIC ADVERTISING

A dynamic ad is specifically curated for a distinct audience based on information known about the audience. So many people are exposed to many different ads.

You’ll oftentimes see dynamic ads, or ones that are specifically for you, online. Have you ever been online shopping and then ads for that shirt you were looking at are now all over your usual sites? That is dynamic advertising!
